# Division Problems with Powers of 10

> [!tip^] Problem 1: A ribbon is 540.6 meters long. You want to cut it into pieces that are each 0.06 meters long. How many pieces can you cut?
> - Solution: To simplify, multiply both 540.6 and 0.06 by \( 10^2 \) (or 100) to eliminate the decimal in the divisor:
>   \[
>   540.6 \div 0.06 = \frac{540.6 \times 100}{0.06 \times 100} = 54060 \div 6 = 9010
>   \]
> - Answer: You can cut 9010 pieces.

> [!tip^] Problem 2: A machine produces 876.3 grams of material each minute. The material is divided into portions of 0.03 grams. How many portions are there in each minute?
> - Solution: Multiply both 876.3 and 0.03 by \( 10^2 \) to simplify the division:
>   \[
>   876.3 \div 0.03 = \frac{876.3 \times 100}{0.03 \times 100} = 87630 \div 3 = 29210
>   \]
> - Answer: There are 29,210 portions.

# Multiplication Problems with Powers of 10

> [!tip^] Problem 1: Each book on a shelf weighs 0.45 kg. If there are 2000 books, what is the total weight?
> - Solution: Multiply \( 0.45 \times 2000 \). To simplify, think of it as shifting the decimal:
>   \[
>   0.45 \times 2000 = 0.45 \times 2 \times 1000 = 0.9 \times 1000 = 900
>   \]
> - Answer: The total weight is 900 kg.

> [!tip^] Problem 2: A small bottle holds 0.034 liters. If you need 1000 bottles to fill a tank, how many liters will it take?
> - Solution: Multiply \( 0.034 \times 1000 \) by shifting the decimal three places to the right:
>   \[
>   0.034 \times 1000 = 34
>   \]
> - Answer: It will take 34 liters.

# Word Problems that Reinforce Decimal Movement

> [!tip^] Problem 1: A rope is 78.4 meters long. You cut it into pieces that are each 0.8 meters long. How many pieces do you have?
> - Solution: \( 78.4 \div 0.8 \). Multiply both by \( 10^1 \) to eliminate the decimal in the divisor:
>   \[
>   78.4 \div 0.8 = \frac{78.4 \times 10}{0.8 \times 10} = 784 \div 8 = 98
>   \]
> - Answer: There are 98 pieces.

> [!tip^] Problem 2: Each tile on a wall covers 0.09 square meters. If a wall has an area of 36 square meters, how many tiles are needed?
> - Solution: \( 36 \div 0.09 \). Multiply both by \( 10^2 \) to eliminate the decimal:
>   \[
>   36 \div 0.09 = \frac{36 \times 100}{0.09 \times 100} = 3600 \div 9 = 400
>   \]
> - Answer: You need 400 tiles.
